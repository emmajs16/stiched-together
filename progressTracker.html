  • conduct market research

    Market research includes gathering information about consumers' needs and preferences to start forming your business plan. Some things to consider include your demand, location, and economic indicators.

  • write your business plan

    This business plan is a document that establishes your company's goals and ambitions as well as the strategies needed to implement them. Two possible formats for these plans are the traditional plan and the lean startup plan.

  • fund your business

    This financing process is essential to establish the money needed for your business to start. Possible sources of funding include self-funding, crowdfunding, investors, grants, and loans.

  • pick your business location

    Throughout picking your business location, you can decided what location and type of facility would be best for your personal business, whether that is online or a physical space. When choosing a location, it is important to consider state and local taxes as well as zoning ordinances.

  • choose your business structure

    The business structure is a form of legal organization for your business that determines key aspects such as taxes, liability, and paperwork. This step is essential before officially registering your business. Some common business structures include sole proprietorships, partnerships, and limited liability companies.

  • choose your business name

    By choosing a name for your business, you are establishing a brand. This name can reflect the company's values, priorities, and mission. Through this process, make sure that this name is not already assigned to a current business.

  • register your business

    Registering your business establishes legal rights for your business. This registration process depends on the business structure of your company as well as the location. In some cases, you might not need to register your business depending on the circumstances.

  • get federal and state tax IDs

    By accessing your Employer Identification Number, you will be able to pay both federal and state taxes. This process is free to apply, but must be done after registering your business.

  • apply for licenses and permits

    Acuquiring the proper licenses and permits are essential for maintaining the legality of your business. These licenses and permits may depend on your location and business activity.

  • open a business bank account

    A business bank account helps you stay legally compliant and protected. It also provides benefits to your customers and employees. Common business accounts include a checking account, savings account, credit card account, and a merchant services account. Once you have selected a bank, simply visit the branch or go online to discover your next steps.
